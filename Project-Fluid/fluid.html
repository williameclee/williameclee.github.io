Fluid Dyanmics

The basic laws that control the system are \begin{align} \frac{\mathrm{D}\boldsymbol{\mathrm{v}}}{\mathrm{D}t} = \frac{\partial\boldsymbol{\mathrm{v}}}{\partial t} + (\boldsymbol{\mathrm{v}}\cdot\nabla)\boldsymbol{\mathrm{v}} &= \nu\nabla^2\boldsymbol{\mathrm{v}} + \boldsymbol{\mathrm{f}} \quad, \label{EQ:force}\tag{1} \end{align} \begin{align} \frac{\mathrm{D}\rho}{\mathrm{D}t} = \frac{\partial\rho}{\partial t} + (\boldsymbol{\mathrm{v}}\cdot\nabla)\rho &= \nu\nabla^2\rho + \boldsymbol{\mathrm{s}} \quad, \label{EQ:density}\tag{2} \end{align} \begin{align} \nabla\cdot\boldsymbol{\mathrm{v}} &= 0 \quad, \label{EQ:continuity}\tag{3} \end{align} where $\boldsymbol{\mathrm{f}}$ is the external force and $\boldsymbol{\mathrm{s}}$ the source of mass (density).

The simulation above follows the paper by Stam, J. (2003) and the JavaScript scripts are partly modified after those kindly made public by Müller, M. (2022).
Press P to pause or restart the simulation, and F to simulate frame-by-frame.
